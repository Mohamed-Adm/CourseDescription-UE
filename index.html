Academic Semester Fall 2018-2019 3rd year of Computer and Communication Engineering CC541 Communication Systems 3 Architecture, technology, operation, and application of telecommunication networks including digital telephony, data networks, and integrated services networks. Design and analysis of networks for voice, data, and video applications, Radio broadcasting and Television. Microwave and satellite links. link budgets, modulation, coding, multiple access techniques, propagation effects, and earth terminals.
CC552 Net-Centric Computing 3 Structure, computer networking applications. Introduction to the World-Wide Web: in TCP/IP. Streams and Datagrams. Internetworking and routing. Network Security. Building web applications: Protocols at the application layer; database-driven web sites; remote procedure calls; lightweight distributed objects; enterprise-wide web based applications Network management. Agents: Introduction to robotic agents; mobile agents
CC472 Sofware Engineering 3 Concepts of Software Development. Life-Cycle of Software. Requirements and Specification. Data Model. Process Model. Design and Coding. Verification, Validation and Testing. Management of Software Projects. Software Evolution
CC474 Switching Theory and Models of Computation 3 Theory of Automata, Regular expression, Models of computability and their applications, Finite state machine, Push-down automaton Turning machine, Petri net.
Academic Semester Spring 2018-2019 3rd year of Computer and Communication Engineering CC441 Analogue Communication Theory 3 Time and frequency division multiplexing, and the design and characterization of linear modulation including amplitude modulation (AM), single sideband, double sideband, and vestigial sideband, as well as nonlinear modulation including frequency modulation (FM), and phase modulation. Superheterodyne receivers. Design includes modulators, detectors, and the effects of noise
CC581 Performance Evaluation 3 Scheduling. Operating system Paging, Buffer, Swaps, Memory resource allocation. Queuing networks. Simulation. Benchmarks...
CC443 Mobile and Wireless Communications 3 Cellular systems technical requirements including analog FM systems such as AMPS and digital systems such as GSM. and CDMA systems. Wireless LAN’s technical requirements such as IEEE802.11. Theory and implementation of OFDM systems
HS402b Introduction to Finance 2 Principles of investments, Financial analysis of corporate projects, Cost of capital, and Capital structure and financing policies.
HS501a Specifications and Feasibility Studies 2 Methods for retrieving quantities, methods for accounting and cost analysis, Management: basics types of projects. Definition of feasibility study, Project development procedure, Project-environment relation, Basic feasibility studies (marketing, regulation, environment, and technical) Comparison of alternatives, Economical analysis, Project evaluation, Applications.
Academic Semester Summer 2018-2019 3rd year of Computer and Communication Engineering EME207 Electric Circuits 3 DC circuit analysis: reduction methods, mesh/loop and node methods, transformation methods, Network theorems. First-order transients. AC circuit analysis: sinusoids and phasors, steady state conditions, impedance and admittance, power and energy, Balanced and unbalanced three-phase circuits. Computer applications.
MP204 Mechanics II 3 Dynamics : introduction in vector analysis, kinematics of bodies, motion analysis in one dimension, relation between force and acceleration, static integration of motion (relation between energy and work), time integration of motion ( relation between impulse and momentum )
